Our Approach

Hypothesis & Experiment-Driven Support

We believe simpler is better. Rather than highly prescriptive guidance, we're testing whether essential, critical information empowers organizations to run Camp AIR on their own.

Jill & Kyran 1:1 — July 9, 2026 · Ref: Mike Phipps — Scalable Service Model

Jill's Hypothesis

“Provide less information, but make sure it is the critical information people need, so they feel empowered to run Camp AIR themselves.”

  • Less-prescriptive guidance → self-sufficiency
  • Too much support → dependency on central team
  • Risk: some orgs may struggle without hands-on help
  • Aligned with Aparna Rao & Tyler Nowlen

Kyran's Approach

Experiment-Driven Support Model

  1. Start with self-service — push orgs to use the kit independently
  2. Stay close & learn from friction — treat support requests as signals
  3. Advisory only when needed — lean in at friction points
  4. AI/agentic support — explore scalable AI-powered guidance
  5. Test before pricing — run with orgs Jul–Dec, then decide
⚠️
FY27 H2 Decision
Based on the feedback loop findings, advisory may remain free, move to a cost-recovery model, or scale via AI agents.

What We're Tracking

Key signals that show whether the self-service model is working, where support is still needed, and how the delivery model should evolve.

📊

Adoption Metrics

  • Orgs using kit independently
  • Orgs requesting advisory
  • Time from kit access → launch
🔍

Friction Points

  • Where orgs get stuck
  • Repeated questions
  • Gaps in the kit
🤖

AI Effectiveness

  • Can AI chatbot resolve queries?
  • Human escalation rate
  • Bot satisfaction
📈

Support Load

  • Hours spent on advisory
  • Is volume manageable?
  • FTE vs vendor effort
💡

Kit Improvements

  • Content updates triggered
  • New templates added
  • User-submitted materials
🎯

Decision Inputs

  • Should advisory stay free?

  • AI vs human support ratio
