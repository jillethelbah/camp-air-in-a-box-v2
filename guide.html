How to Run Camp AIR

Your step-by-step guide to delivering an AI immersion experience for your team or organization

⏱️ 4-6 weeks from start to finish

Camp AIR can start small or launch across an organization. The right delivery model helps you size the effort, recruit the right people, and set realistic expectations from day one.

Direct team

Manager-Led

You're the manager, running it for your direct team (5-30 people). You own facilitation. Lightest lift.

  • Best when you know your participants and their workflows well
  • Faster decision-making and easier scheduling
  • Great starting point for a first cohort
Cross-org delivery

V-Team-Led

Cross-org virtual team assembles to deliver to a larger org (50-200+ people). Needs sponsors, facilitators, and coordination.

  • Works well for multi-team or function-wide rollouts
  • Requires stronger comms, logistics, and sponsor visibility
  • Ideal when demand is already established
Organic expansion

Scale-from-Within

Grow from a successful team experience into broader org adoption. Organic, champion-driven.

  • Build on momentum from an existing successful camp
  • Create champions who can co-facilitate future cohorts
  • Useful when other teams are asking to join
Which model is right for me? ⌄
“My team is 10 people and I'm their manager”
→ Manager-Led
“My VP wants this for 200 engineers across 5 teams”
→ V-Team-Led
“One team loved it and now others want in”
→ Scale-from-Within

0 of 7 setup tasks complete

Great camps feel smooth because facilitator prep happened before the first participant joins. Use this week to build confidence, align roles, and get your communications out early.

What to prepare

  • Train-the-Trainer (TtT): Facilitators attend a structured enablement session to learn the material and delivery approach.
  • What facilitators receive: Facilitator lesson plans with timing, session flows and prompts, learner guidance materials, and practice environment access.
  • Manager Kickoff Prep: Managers attend a prep meeting to understand their role in supporting AI-first teams.
  • Gemba Walk Planning: Leads attend orientation to plan workflow observations.
  • Comms: Send participant welcome emails with schedule, expectations, and pre-work.

Facilitator readiness checklist

  • Dry-run the opening kickoff and transitions
  • Clarify who owns demos, labs, Q&A, and attendance
  • Verify links, environments, and participant materials
  • Establish an escalation path for blockers during the cohort

The goal is not just content consumption — it is real adoption. Keep the rhythm tight, make time for practice, and connect learning directly to real workflows.

Week 1 — Immersion Week

4-5 days of live learning + labs

Set the foundation with mindset, tooling, and hands-on practice.

Day 1
Kickoff, Mindset & Workflow, GH Copilot App, GH Copilot CLI
Day 2
Recap, MCP Servers, Clawpilot/Scout
Day 3
Agency, 1ES Tools, Bluebird, Vibe Coding
Day 4
Responsible AI, Evaluation Workshop
Day 5
Hackathon Prep, Previous Cohort Demos
  • Format: Live sessions + hands-on practice labs
Week 2 — Hackathon Week

Apply learning to real work

  • Teams apply learnings to real workflows
  • Gemba Walk deep dives with Workforce Transformation experts
  • Weekly check-ins for advisory-supported orgs
Week 3 — Demo Days

Show what changed

  • Teams present their hackathon projects
  • Celebrate wins, share learnings
  • Executive readout (for V-Team-Led)

Pro Tips for Delivery

  • Record all sessions for the async library
  • Designate a Slack/Teams channel for real-time Q&A
  • Have participants buddy up for practice labs
  • Keep energy high — vary formats (demo, hands-on, discussion)

The most effective camps make outcomes visible. Use a lightweight measurement loop so leaders understand the impact and future cohorts get better each time.

Close the loop

  • Post-Cohort Survey: Send within 48 hours of Demo Day
  • Debrief: Run a facilitator retrospective — what worked, what to improve
  • Executive Readout: Share impact summary with leadership
  • Next Steps: Plan your next cohort or expand to more teams
