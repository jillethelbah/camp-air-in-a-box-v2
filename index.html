Step 1 — Who Are You Delivering To?

Choose Your Delivery Scope

Pick the delivery path that matches how you want Camp AIR to spread — directly through a manager, centrally through a V-team, or by building internal teachers who scale it from within.

👥
Manager-Led Camp AIR
Run Camp AIR for my own team.

A leader wants to upskill an intact team directly. Scale mechanism: Manager sponsorship + team participation.

  • 10–30 people in one intact team
  • Manager sponsorship + team participation
  • Lightweight coordination with a pre-built kit
  • Fast path from kickoff to delivery
🏢
V-Team-Led Camp AIR
Run Camp AIR across my organization.

A cross-org group can coordinate delivery for many teams. Scale mechanism: Centralized facilitation across a broad group.

  • 50–500+ people across multiple teams
  • Centralized facilitation across a broad group
  • Multi-cohort scheduling, registration, and comms
  • Shared reporting, governance, and sponsor alignment
🌱
Scale-from-Within Camp AIR
Build internal teachers who can run Camp AIR for their peers.

An org wants durable, repeatable AI capacity inside existing teams. Scale mechanism: Train-the-trainers — Pioneers become Coaches and create the next wave.

  • Internal teachers seeded inside participating teams
  • Train-the-trainers model that compounds over time
  • Pioneers co-lead delivery and sustain adoption after camp
  • Real-work application tied to team workflows

Choose Your Delivery Model

How Do You Want to Run Camp AIR?

Select the model that fits your scale. Each comes with a complete operational playbook — from registration to measurement.

👥
Manager-Led
10–30 people · 1 team/group

Run a focused Camp AIR experience for a single team. Ideal for managers who want to upskill their immediate team quickly with minimal coordination overhead.

  • Lightweight setup — manager-led coordination
  • Pre-built 3-week agenda & lab selection guide
  • Self-contained facilitator kit
  • Built-in measurement & feedback templates
🏢
V-Team-Led (Org/Division)
50–500+ people · Multi-team coordination

Coordinate Camp AIR across many teams through a central V-team or program office. Best when you need consistent delivery, shared operations, and visible impact across a broad group.

  • Multi-cohort scheduling, registration, and cohort orchestration
  • Facilitator enablement pipeline with Train-the-Trainer support
  • Centralized comms, stakeholder alignment, and executive sponsorship
  • Cross-team measurement, dashboards, and impact reporting
🌱
Scale-from-Within
Pioneers, Coaches & peer teachers · Durable scaling

Build internal teachers who can run Camp AIR for their peers — creating a self-sustaining network of Pioneers, Coaches, and change-makers.

  • Pioneer nomination & preparation before camp
  • Pioneers co-facilitate alongside SMEs during camp
  • Post-camp sustainment — Pioneers become peer teachers
  • Self-scaling model — each cohort creates the next wave of teachers
  • Real-work application tied to actual team workflows

Operational Playbooks

What's Inside Each Model

Expand each section to see the details. Full playbooks with step-by-step guides are coming soon.

👥
Manager-Led — Team Delivery
10–30 participants · 3 weeks · Lightweight ops
▼

The Manager-Led model is designed for a single manager to bring Camp AIR to their team with minimal overhead. The manager acts as the coordinator, using a pre-packaged facilitator kit and selecting from curated lab options.

  • Who runs it: Team manager + 1 facilitator (can be the same person)
  • Registration: Simple sign-up via Teams or email — no formal registration system needed
  • Labs: Pick 3–4 from the curated non-engineering or engineering lab catalog
  • Facilitation: Facilitator lesson plans included — no TtT required at this scale
  • Measurement: Pre/post survey templates + team impact summary
  • Timeline: 1 week to prep, 3 weeks to deliver, 1 week to wrap

📝 Detailed playbook with templates, timelines, and checklists — coming soon.

🏢
V-Team-Led (Org/Division) — Central Coordination
50–500+ participants · Multiple cohorts · Coordinated ops at scale
▼

The V-Team-Led model combines the organization and division playbooks into one centralized operating model. A cross-org team coordinates cohorts, facilitator readiness, stakeholder communications, and reporting so many teams can move together without each team reinventing the rollout.

  • Who runs it: Program lead or V-team + ops support + facilitator pool + sponsor alignment
  • Registration: Centralized registration form with cohort, wave, and team assignment
  • Labs: Consistent lab track across cohorts with governed customization by audience
  • Facilitation: Train-the-Trainer sessions, facilitator pipeline, and quality assurance for consistent delivery
  • Comms: Centralized announcements, reminders, manager enablement, and wrap-up templates
  • Sponsorship: Executive and manager sponsor motions to reinforce why the rollout matters
  • Measurement: Cross-cohort dashboards, stakeholder readouts, ROI framing, and showcase reporting
  • Governance: Shared content review, versioning, and operational checklists for scale
  • Timeline: 3–6 weeks to prep depending on scope, 3-week cohort delivery, then ongoing measurement and follow-on waves

📝 Detailed playbook with templates, timelines, and checklists — coming soon.

🌱
Scale-from-Within — Pioneer-to-Coach Model
3–4 weeks prep · 3 weeks delivery · Ongoing sustainment
▼

Scale-from-Within starts before the formal camp begins. Each participating team nominates people with credibility, curiosity, and peer influence. These Pioneers are prepared in advance to learn the Camp AIR methods, practice facilitation, adapt materials to their team context, and co-lead delivery.

  • Leadership sponsorship: Leaders protect time, clarify why AI empowerment matters
  • Pioneer nomination: Teams identify people who can become credible peer teachers
  • Trainer preparation: Pioneers learn Camp AIR methods, facilitation moves before camp
  • Real-work application: Activities connect to actual workflows, not abstract demos
  • Post-camp sustainment: Pioneers keep teaching, sharing patterns, identifying next wave

Operating model: Before camp → During camp → After camp cycle

Timeline: 3–4 weeks prep (includes Pioneer enablement), 3 weeks delivery, ongoing sustainment
